# Qubic Capital Governance Proposal  
## Permissionless Liquidity Pool Creation for QCAP

## Background

A discussion has emerged within the QSWAP community about allowing **any participant to create liquidity pools for any token**, instead of restricting pool creation to the **token issuer**.

This approach would align QSWAP with the **fully permissionless model used by many decentralized exchanges**, where users can freely create pools for any asset pair.

Because this potential change could affect **QCAP**, and because **Qubic Capital is a major shareholder of QSWAP**, we believe it is important to consult Qubic Capital voters on this topic.

---

## Position of the Qubic Capital Representatives

In the interest of transparency, we would like to share the current view of the Qubic Capital representatives.

After internal discussion, **all seven representatives currently support voting NO on enabling permissionless liquidity pool creation for QCAP at this stage of the project**.

This proposal is still submitted for a community vote, but we believe it is important to share the reasoning behind this position so voters can make an informed decision.

---

## Key Considerations

### Limited circulating supply

Currently **only around 15% of the total QCAP supply is in circulation**, with the remaining tokens distributed progressively through **project fundraising rounds**.

At this stage, liquidity pools would likely begin with **very low liquidity**, making prices highly sensitive to relatively small trades.

---

### Early-stage liquidity can produce unstable price signals

In liquidity pools, **price is determined by the ratio of assets in the pool**.  
When liquidity is shallow, relatively small trades can move prices significantly, creating **price signals that may not reflect the broader value of the project**.

---

### Fundraising is currently the main value-creation mechanism

QCAP distribution currently happens through **structured fundraising rounds**.

These rounds both **distribute tokens** and **bring capital directly into the project**.  
If permissionless pools were introduced now, they would create **an additional way to acquire QCAP**, potentially competing with these fundraising mechanisms.

Since fundraising is currently **the primary way QCAP purchases generate value for the project**, protecting its effectiveness is an important consideration.

---

### Existing liquidity options

It is also worth noting that **QX already provides a mechanism for holders who wish to exit their position**, meaning some level of liquidity already exists today.

---

## Summary

Permissionless liquidity pools can be a valuable feature in decentralized exchanges and may make sense **once a token is more widely distributed and deeper liquidity exists**.

However, with **only ~15% of QCAP currently in circulation**, the Qubic Capital representatives believe it may be **premature to introduce permissionless pools for QCAP at this stage**.

For this reason, the current position of the representatives is to **vote NO**, while leaving the final decision to the community through this vote.

---

## Possible Alternatives

If the community wishes to move toward a more permissionless model in QSWAP, a potential compromise could be:

- **Default permissionless pools (opt-in by default)** for all tokens.
- **Issuer opt-out capability**, allowing specific tokens to disable permissionless pool creation if their distribution model requires it.

This approach would preserve the **open nature of the exchange**, while still allowing projects with **early-stage token distribution or structured fundraising models** to protect their market structure until their token supply is more widely distributed.

---

## Voting

- **Vote YES** to allow **permissionless liquidity pool creation** for tokens on QSWAP, allowing anyone to create a QCAP liquidity pool. 
- **Vote NO** to **keep pool creation restricted to the token issuer** at this stage.  
  This option also covers those who support exploring **alternative compromise solutions**, such as allowing tokens to opt out of permissionless pool creation while keeping it enabled by default for others.
